# Natief

 “**An introduction to sample preparation and imaging by cryo-electron microscopy for structural biology**
 
 […]
 
 3.2. Vitrification
 
 Sample preparation techniques such as staining and plastic embedding involve the dehydration of biological specimens, which fundamentally removes them from **their native**, aqueous **environment**. 
 
 […]
 
 3. Grid preparation
 
 Once the specimen is ready, it must be prepared for imaging by EM. To image with electrons, a microscope is maintained under a high vacuum, in which hydrated biological specimens would dehydrate rapidly. Thus to image a biological specimen, it must be fixed, preferably **in a native (or native-like) state**.”
 
 –**https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4854231/** (2020-03-18)
