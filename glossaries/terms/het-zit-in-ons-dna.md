# het zit in ons DNA

- *het zit in ons DNA** is a Dutch business cliché commonly used by companies that want to suggest a particular quality is so deeply ingrained in their organisation that it’s practically genetic. It is the corporate world’s way of saying: “we’ve always done it this way, and we always will,” but with a touch of biotech flair.  

1. ## Usage
Marketeers love this phrase when talking about values such as *innovatie*, *duurzaamheid* or *klantgerichtheid*. It suggests inevitability: just as you can’t change your chromosomes, you can’t stop this company from being, say, *innovatief*.  

- Voorbeeld: *Duurzaamheid zit in ons DNA.*  
- Vertaling: *Sustainability is in our DNA.*  
- Cynical translation: *We really hope you’ll believe this because we printed it on our website.*  

1. ## English equivalents
- *It’s in our DNA* (the same cliché exists in English)  
- *Part of our DNA*  
- *Genetically coded into the company* (rare, tongue-in-cheek)  

1. ## Notes
While it works in Dutch and English, overuse has made it a corporate bingo square alongside *out of the box* and *synergy*. If you must translate it, keep it short and cliché-for-cliché: *It’s in our DNA*. For a wry aside, translators sometimes prefer: *It’s kind of our thing.*
