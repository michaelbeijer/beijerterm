# striklint

- **Dutch**: striklint
- **English**: tie belt, sash, or ribbon belt

----

The term **"striklint"** in Dutch refers to a **tie belt**, **sash**, or **ribbon belt**, depending on the garment and context. It usually describes a belt or strip of fabric designed to be tied into a bow or knot, commonly seen on dresses, blouses, or coats.

1. ## Translation Options:

1. **Tie belt**
1. * Commonly used for garments with a functional belt that can be tied.
1. * Example: "A dress with a tie belt at the waist."
1. **Sash**
1. * Often used for wider, decorative belts tied around the waist.
1. * Example: "The coat features a sash for a cinched silhouette."
1. **Ribbon belt**
1. * Suitable for narrower or more delicate belts, often decorative.
1. * Example: "The blouse is styled with a ribbon belt."

1. ## Contextual Recommendation:

- For **practical garments** (e.g., a dress or coat), **tie belt** is the most idiomatic.
- For **decorative or formal pieces**, such as gowns, **sash** works better.
