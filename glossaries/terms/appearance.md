1. # Dutch
- aanblik
- aanzicht (more about how something looks from a specific viewpoint)
- aanzien (This word means "look," "aspect," or "regard." It can be used for the appearance of objects, but it often carries a connotation of prestige or status.)
- aspect (A loanword from French/English, commonly used in Dutch technical contexts)
- intrede (in gebruik komen)
- optreden
- uiterlijk (referring to the visual properties of a physical thing; the most direct and common translation of "appearance", generally meaning "outward aspect" or "look")
- uiterlijke kenmerken (translates to "external characteristics" or "visual characteristics")
- [COMP.] verschijning
- **Van Dale English-Dutch**:
- * verschijnsel **(fenomeen)** 
- * optreden **(verschijning)** 
- ** *he made his last appearance = hij trad voor de laatste keer op*
- ** *he put in/made an appearance at the party = hij liet zich even zien/gaf acte de présence op het feest*
- voorkomen, vóórkomen (translates to "appearance," but can sometimes imply a more general impression, including behaviour or demeanor, especially when referring to people)
- verschijningsvorm (referring to the form in which something appears)
- visuele eigenschappen (translates to visual characteristics)
