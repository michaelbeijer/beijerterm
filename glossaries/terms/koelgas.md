# koelgas

- *koelgas** is a Dutch technical term referring to a gas used as a heat-transfer medium to remove heat from a liquid, typically by circulating through an immersed pipe assembly or coil. Unlike *koelmiddel* (coolant/refrigerant), *koelgas* does not imply a refrigeration cycle or phase change; it specifically denotes a gas used for sensible heat transfer.

1. # Preferred English translation
- *cooling gas**

This is the most accurate, literal, and legally safe translation in patent contexts.

1. # Definition
In Dutch engineering and patent terminology, *koelgas* means a gas used for cooling by flowing through heat-exchange tubing or coils. It is distinct from:
- *koelmiddel* – coolant or refrigerant (may imply phase change)
- *koudemiddel* – refrigerant (refrigeration technology)
- *koelvloeistof* – cooling liquid / coolant liquid

1. # Usage in patents
- Koelgas* appears in Dutch patents describing:
- pipe-coil heat exchangers  
- CO₂-based cooling loops  
- immersion cooling systems  
- industrial liquid-cooling apparatus  

Example:
<blockquote>“… een samenstel van leidingen voor het transport van koelgas …”</blockquote>
→ *“an assembly of pipes for transporting cooling gas …”*
