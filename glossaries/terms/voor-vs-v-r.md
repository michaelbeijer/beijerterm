# voor vs vóór

In Dutch, "voor" can mean "for" (purposive), but it can also mean "before" (temporal). However, if you want to be clear, you can write the temporal version as "vóór". I highly recommend patent drafters to **always** use "vóór" if they mean before, since it isn't always clear from the context, possibly saving time (and money) down the road since translators like myself end up wasting their time figuring out which the author meant.

<blockquote>voor het storten van het beton = 

1. before pouring the concrete
1. for pouring the concrete
vóór het storten van het beton = 

1. before pouring the concrete
</blockquote>
