# gezag van gewijsde

*"Gezag van gewijsde"* refers to the **binding force of a final court decision** (res judicata). It means that a judgment is legally conclusive and cannot be re-litigated between the same parties on the same subject matter. In common law terms, it corresponds to **res judicata**, which prevents the reopening of a case that has already been finally adjudicated.

 **Res judicata** or res iudicata, also known as claim preclusion, is the Latin term for judged matter,[1] and refers to either of two concepts in common law civil procedure: 
 
 (1) a case in which there has been a final judgment and that is no longer subject to appeal; 
 (2) and the legal doctrine meant to bar (or preclude) relitigation of a claim between the same parties.
 
 In the case of res judicata, the matter cannot be raised again, either in the same court or in a different court. A court will use res judicata to deny reconsideration of a matter. The doctrine of res judicata is a method of preventing injustice to the parties of a case supposedly finished but perhaps also or mostly a way of avoiding unnecessary waste of judicial resources. Res judicata does not merely prevent future judgments from contradicting earlier ones, but also prevents litigants from multiplying judgments.
