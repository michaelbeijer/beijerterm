# taper

1. # Oxford Dictionaries Premium
 taper (verb):
 **1.** diminish or reduce in thickness towards one end: ‘David asked my dressmaker to taper his trousers’
 
 **1.1** (taper off) [object](no) 
 gradually lessen: ‘the impact of the dollar's depreciation started to taper off’
 
 **Synonyms**: narrow, thin (out), become narrow, become narrower, become thin, become thinner, come to a point, attenuate

1. # Watch out! Sometimes, ‘toelopen’/‘aflopen’ are used differently
 For example, in a patent I am translating, they are defined as follows:
 
 **taps toelopen / taps toeloopt / loopt taps toe** =
 tapers with increasing width/thickness (◀)
 tapers to a thicker dimension
 thickens towards its distal end
 **reverse-taper, reverse-tapered**
 
 **taps aflopen / taps afloopt / loopt taps af** = 
 tapers with decreasing width/thickness (▶)
 tapers to a narrower dimension
 narrows towards its distal end
 **tapers, is tapered**
