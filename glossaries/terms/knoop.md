<blockquote>
- *Nubs & Slubs**

Linen, hemp & wool are two yarns which are well-known for having little **nubs and slubs** along the length of the yarn.

Slubs occur when a yarn does not have a consistent diameter along the whole length of the thread. Where the yarn is thicker it interacts with the warp yarns differently causing visible slubs.

- *Nubs** may appear as little lumps in the fabric or sometimes as little loops. These loops may look like pulls but they are actually a natural part of the yarn structure. They differ from a pull in that they are thin and a little wispy, and do not affect the fabric around them â€“ i.e. there is no tension in the area around them. These can be left as they will not affect the integrity of the sling.
